Part 1: Text Summary

The story takes place initially in Calcutta where one day the trio of Feluda, his nephew Tapesh and the thriller writer Lalmohan Ganguli are approached by Ranjan at Feluda’s home. Ranjan shows Feluda a photograph of himself with another boy when they were young. The boy was determined to be not Indian due to his white and pale skin. Ranjan studied in England before he met with an accident that took away his memories. He couldn't recall who the boy in the photograph is and wants him to be found. This starts Feluda’s journey of meeting a plethora of people and taking their accounts of Ranjan’s past and building the story. When there’s no more information to be found in India, they travel to London to visit Ranjan’s school and college. They’re finally able to uncover the missing boy’s identity and his beliefs.

Part 3: Meaning and payoff

Genre of thriller has the ability to grip the reader to every event that takes place in a story. The way our story begins, like any other detective story, a reader has no idea about the direction it is gonna take and the events that are gonna follow. This creates difficulty in seeing the complete picture painted by these events. This is what my map aims to achieve, putting forward a summarization of the text through abstraction and using only the most important elements of the story, the characters. But it’s also not just the characters in isolation that’s important in creating this picture but also how each character complements the other and helps in completing the puzzle the detective solves. In this case, the puzzle was uncovering Peter’s identity. Here, the word ‘identity’ is important since it’s not the face in the photograph which is already there but what Ranjan’s relationship is with Peter and what Peter stood for. Peter’s face is not seen in the puzzle since that becomes irrelevant when Peter’s own beliefs dictate that skin colour determines a person’s identity and most of the characters remember him as a racist.

The people who helped uncover the client’s identity are placed at the left side of the puzzle and the right side for Peter. The characters’ proximity to the two figures also shows their importance in uncovering the identity and finally, the redness represents the violent undertones of the story.

Part 4: Data Collection

The main emphasis while creating the map was on abstraction. For this, I began the process by collecting all the elements of the story that seemed relevant in summarizing or providing insights into the text. These elements were all the characters, relevant locations where events took place and, finally movements that capture the relationship of locations and the characters. In this particular text, there was an overwhelming amount of characters that hinted at what’s given a lot of importance in the story. Once the important element has been identified, what’s left is to connect the element to the identity of the story. For instance, the identity of the chosen text cannot be the fun Feluda and his group had in London since that’s not what the reader is reading the text for. It should be what the reader was promised at the beginning, the identity of the missing boy.
